# WisWealthy — Impeccable Design Context

## Design Context

### Users

**Primary:** High Net Worth Individuals (HNIs) and sophisticated investors in India, aged roughly 40–65. They are time-scarce, financially literate, and skeptical of salesmanship. They have been pitched to many times and can immediately sense when advice is commission-driven. They are likely browsing on a laptop, not a phone, and they are making a significant trust decision, not a casual purchase.

**Secondary:** Referrals — someone's CA, family member, or trusted friend sends them this link. The site must work as a credibility artifact, not just a landing page.

**Context of use:** Deliberate, unhurried. This is not a scroll-and-forget site. The visitor arrives with intent, reads carefully, and looks for signals of depth and integrity.

**Job to be done:** "Is this someone I can trust to advise me on multi-crore decisions? Do they think the way I think?"

### Brand Personality

**Three words:** Warm, rigorous, plain-spoken

- **Warm** — Not cold or corporate. There's a real person (Samir) behind this, with a long history of relationships. Express warmth through generous whitespace, approachable copy, and language that treats the reader as an intelligent adult — not through round icons, pastels, or exclamation marks.
- **Rigorous** — Every claim is earned. The site should feel like it was written by someone who doesn't need to impress you, because their record speaks for itself. No superlatives. No "we are the best." Just clear, considered statements.
- **Plain-spoken** — No jargon. No em dashes (compliance rule). Short sentences. If a sentence can be cut, cut it.

### Emotional Goal

The primary emotional response on landing should be: **"These people clearly know what they're doing."**

This is achieved through:
- Typography that is confident and legible, not decorative
- Copy that doesn't try too hard
- A design that feels composed and deliberate, not busy
- Gold accents that feel earned, not scattered

**Secondary emotion:** A quiet invitation — "I'm curious to know more."

### Aesthetic Direction

**Theme:** Dark-first. Deep navy base, charcoal surface, warm gold accent. The darkness signals seriousness and premium positioning without coldness; the gold provides warmth and authority.

**Brand tokens:**
- Deep Navy: `oklch(0.14 0.04 240)` — primary background
- Charcoal: `oklch(0.22 0.04 235)` — card/surface background
- Ivory: `oklch(0.96 0.01 80)` — for light text and accent use
- Warm Gold: `oklch(0.72 0.12 75)` — primary accent, CTAs, labels

**Current fonts (v1):** Syne Variable (headings) + DM Sans Variable (body).
> ⚠️ **Future iteration note:** Both Syne and DM Sans are in the impeccable reflex-reject list. For the next design pass, explore alternatives. For headings: consider a geometric grotesque with personality (Euclid Circular, Aktiv Grotesk, or Roc Grotesk from Pangram Pangram). For body: something with a slight humanist quality that still reads clean at small sizes (Biotif, Neue Haas Grotesk, or Söhne).

**Anti-references (what NOT to look like):**
- Generic fintech: blue-heavy, feature-grid layouts, stock photography of skylines
- Over-designed luxury: excessive animation, marble textures, ostentatious gold gradients
- AI-generated look: glowing neon borders, gradient text, sparklines as decoration
- Traditional Indian wealth firms: dated serif-heavy, image-heavy, trust-badge-cluttered

### Design Principles

1. **Competence through restraint.** Impress through precision, not decoration. Remove anything that doesn't add meaning. A sparse page with the right words is more impressive than a busy page with the right icons.

2. **Gold is a truth signal, not a decoration.** The warm gold accent appears where it counts: pill badges, CTAs, hover states, year markers in the timeline. Never scattered. Never gradients. When everything is gold, nothing is.

3. **The dark surface must breathe.** Dark-first doesn't mean dense. Generous vertical spacing (80–120px between sections) is non-negotiable. The glow-hero radial gradient provides atmosphere without adding elements.

4. **Typography is the design.** With no photography and placeholder brand assets, the type does all the work. Headings should be bold, tight, and immediate. Body should be small, readable, and unhurried. The contrast between them is the hierarchy.

5. **Every word is a fiduciary act.** The compliance rules (no "Wealth Management", no em dashes) are symptoms of a deeper truth: this firm is careful. The design should feel careful too — not rigid, but intentional. Never lorem ipsum, never filler text.

### Technical Constraints

- **Framework:** Astro 5 (SSG) + React 19 + Tailwind CSS v4
- **Components:** shadcn/ui + Radix UI
- **Icons:** Lucide React only
- **No CMS:** All content is hardcoded (v1). Keystatic may be added in v2.
- **Compliance:** Never use "Wealth Management" anywhere on the site. No em dashes.
- **Embeds:** Cal.com (scheduling) and Tally (forms) — currently placeholders, real IDs to be added.
- **Deployment:** Vercel
